# When to Seek Professional Mental Health Help

## Signs That Professional Support May Be Helpful

- **Persistent low mood or sadness** lasting more than two weeks.
- **Anxiety** that gets in the way of work, relationships, or daily tasks.
- **Changes in sleep or appetite** that last and affect your wellbeing.
- **Withdrawing** from people and activities you used to enjoy.
- **Difficulty concentrating**, remembering things, or making decisions.
- **Irritability or anger** that feels hard to control or out of character.
- **Physical symptoms** (e.g. headaches, stomach problems, fatigue) that don’t have a clear physical cause.
- **Thoughts of self-harm, suicide, or not wanting to be here** — these always warrant immediate professional support.

## Types of Professional Support

**General Practitioner (GP)**: A good first step. Can assess your overall health, discuss options, and refer you to mental health services if needed.

**Counsellors and psychologists**: Offer talking therapies (e.g. CBT, supportive counselling) for anxiety, depression, stress, and other concerns.

**Psychiatrists**: Medical doctors who can diagnose mental health conditions and prescribe medication when appropriate.

**Crisis and helpline services**: For moments of crisis or when you need to talk to someone straight away. In Singapore, 24/7 options include the National Care Hotline and Samaritans of Singapore.

Seeking help is a sign of strength. Early support often leads to better outcomes.
