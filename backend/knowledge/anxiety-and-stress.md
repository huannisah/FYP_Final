# Understanding Anxiety and Stress

## What Are Anxiety and Stress?

**Stress** is the body’s response to demands or pressure. Some stress is normal and can be motivating. When it’s ongoing or overwhelming, it can affect mood, sleep, and health.

**Anxiety** is worry, nervousness, or fear that can be mild or intense. It often involves physical symptoms (e.g. racing heart, tight chest, restlessness, trouble sleeping). When it’s frequent or strong enough to affect daily life, it may be an anxiety condition that benefits from support.

## Common Physical and Emotional Signs

- Restlessness or feeling on edge
- Tiredness or difficulty concentrating
- Irritability
- Muscle tension or headaches
- Changes in sleep or appetite
- Avoidance of situations that trigger anxiety
- Racing thoughts or constant worry

## What Helps

**Lifestyle**: Regular sleep, balanced meals, and gentle physical activity can reduce anxiety and stress over time.

**Reducing triggers**: Limiting caffeine, alcohol, and excessive news or social media can help some people.

**Relaxation and grounding**: Breathing exercises, grounding techniques (e.g. 5-4-3-2-1), and short mindfulness practices can calm the nervous system in the moment.

**Talking**: Sharing with someone you trust or a helpline can ease the sense of carrying everything alone.

**Professional support**: If anxiety or stress is affecting your life, a GP, counsellor, or psychologist can offer assessment and evidence-based treatment (e.g. therapy, sometimes medication).

## Important Note

This information is for general awareness only. It is not a diagnosis or treatment. If you are in crisis or having thoughts of self-harm or suicide, please contact a crisis helpline or emergency service immediately.
